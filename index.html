THE SIGNATURES

Asam Laksa

A sour and spicy noodle soup in tamarind broth, topped with mackerel flakes, cucumber, pineapple, and mint for a bold, refreshing flavor.

  • Fishy Flavour
  • Fresh Herbs
  • Sour Kick
Char Kuey Teow

Flat rice noodles stir-fried over high heat with prawns, egg, bean sprouts, and chives, infused with the smoky aroma of “wok hei.”

  • Wok Hei
  • Savory Bite
  • Umami Burst
Hokkien Mee

Yellow noodles and vermicelli in a rich, red prawn broth, pork slices, hard-boiled egg, and a spoonful of spicy sambal for extra heat.

  • Spicy Flavour
  • Contain Seafood
  • Bold Aroma

THE MUST-GO
RESTAURANT

Ghee Lian Green Tomyum

A hidden gem in George Town serving the first green tom yum in Penang a beautifully balanced bowl of sour-spicy broth with lime, fresh grouper, mushrooms, tomatoes, crispy fried fish and plump prawns.

Penang Road Famous Laksa

Iconic Penang asam laksa with thick rice noodles bathed in a tangy, sardine-infused broth made from hand-deboned fish heads and bones, tamarind, and chili paste—rich, balanced, and unforgettable.
Ah Leng Char Kway Teow

A celebrated stall (since 1979) specializing in stir-fried flat rice noodles infused with smoky “wok-hei,” topped with jumbo prawns (or mantis prawns in the special version) and optional creamy duck egg.

Super Hokkien Mee

A famed Penang prawn noodle soup with a rich seafood broth simmered from prawn heads, pork ribs, and dried shrimp, served over noodles-packed with flavor and topped with prawns and pork slices.

Super Star Koay Teow Soup

A beloved and Michelin-listed hawker gem serving deeply savory, slow-simmered chicken broth loaded with shredded chicken, pork slices, fish balls, pork liver—and tender braised chicken feet.
Nasi Kandar Beratur

A nighttime favorite known for generous portions of flavorful curry 'banjir' over rice, tender fried chicken, and rich gravies—served only late evening, drawing long queues of eager locals and tourists
